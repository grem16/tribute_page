Charles Babbage

Charles Babbage (1791-1871) was an English mathematician and engineer who developed the first mechanical calculator. His intention was to make calculations more reliable, which were essential for different sectors of industry to avoid potentially fatal errors.

He was one of the first scientists to have a rough idea of ​​what we know today as a computer.

Towards the end of the 18th century and beginning of the 19th century, the development of mathematics was well established by scientists who left solid foundations for geometry, trigonometry, algebra, etc. However, the calculations were significantly tedious and complex to execute, which often led to errors.

He then designed and built his mechanical calculator, capable of performing numerical functions using the difference method.
